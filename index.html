FAQ's

A fire damage restoration service typically includes a thorough assessment of the damage, removal of fire-damaged materials, smoke and soot cleanup, water damage restoration (if firefighting efforts have caused water damage), odor removal, and repairs to affected structures. This process ensures that both the visible and hidden damage from fire, smoke, and water is properly addressed.

Fire damage restoration services can often begin immediately after the fire department clears the property for entry. Many restoration companies offer emergency services to start cleanup and repairs quickly, as addressing fire and smoke damage as soon as possible helps prevent further damage and reduces the risk of mold or long-term structural issues.

In most cases, homeowners' insurance policies cover fire damage repair, including the costs of restoring the structure and contents. However, coverage depends on the specifics of your policy. It's a good idea to contact your insurance provider to understand your coverage and ensure the restoration company works with your insurance to make the process smoother.
